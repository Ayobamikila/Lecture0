Computer Graphics

Computer graphics is the discipline of generating images with the aid of computers. Today, computer graphics is a core technology in digital photography, film, video games, cell phone and computer display, and many specialized applications. A great deal of specialized hardware and software has been developed, with the displays of most devices being driven by computer graphics hardware. It is a vast and recently developed area of computer science. The phrase was coined in 1960 by computer graphics researchers Verne Hudson and William Fetter of Boeing. It is often abbreviated as CG, or typically in the context of film as CGI.

Computer Graphics Design

Computer graphics design combines skills in technology, business and art, using computer generated images and words to create unique concepts and messages for publishing and advertising. Computer graphic designing means making computer-generated images and design different concepts to make images look more beautiful and attractive. It includes a number of tools like Adobe, Autodesk, etc. These softwares make our work easier and faster once we learn the tool.

Applications Of computer Graphics Design

Digital Design

Whenever you see an image that is being manipulated or changed, that is digital design in action. In this subspecialty, you focus on the aesthetics and perception of the image you create. Artwork and images created for an advertising campaign or a billboard are often the work of a digital designer.

Multimedia Design

The presentation of images and information on a website is encompassed in multimedia design. Multimedia design can be used to combine timing and sound with text and images to grab your attention and explain a concept. Computer software programs such as adobe premiere pro can be used to create animation or videos, which are a form of computer graphic design.

Type Design (Typography)

Often you might skim over the words in an ad or just read the text for its direct meaning, without noticing the type face that was used. The formal construction of words in an advertisement may not receive much notice by the reader, but it has an impact on how the image is perceived. By working in type design, you draw and present letters and words that are aesthetically pleasing or unique in order to draw more attention to your message.

Portfolio

Building a great portfolio is very crucial in getting hired. Using completed school project is an easy way to flesh out your portfolio. You can also add computer graphic designs you have created in your off time to your portfolio and show your work to an instructor for some feedback. Additionally, participating in an internship or externship activities may provide you with some practical experience in the field as well as the opportunities to build up your portfolio.

Career Opportunities

As a computer graphic designer, you have several career options to choose from. If you are solely interested in expressing yourself creatively you might want to become an artist. If you are more interested in helping a company, business or an organization convey a message to an audience, then you might want to pursue a career as a graphic designer. In this career you have to consult with consult with your client to understand the message they want to send and identify the target audience and then create appropriate media. According to the U.S bureau of labour statistics (BLS), the expected overall job growth rate for graphic designers from 2016-2026 was 4%, though opportunities should vary widely by industry. For example, jobs as computer system analyst were forecast to increase by 9%. As of 2018 graphic designers earned a median salary of $50,370. If you are more interested in organizing and formatting existing images and information, then you may want to consider a career as a desktop publisher. These professionals decide on the arrangements of text and images for brochures, calendars, newspapers, newsletters, magazines and books. However, you should be aware that a 14% decrease in employment is predicted for the 2016-2026 decade. In 2018, desktop publishers earned a median annual wage of $42,910 all per the BLS.
